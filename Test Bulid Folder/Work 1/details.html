HAMPI, INDIA.

Hampi was the magnificent capital of the Vijayanagara Empire (1336-1565),
a powerful South Indian Hindu kingdom, renowned for its immense wealth,
stunning temples, and sophisticated urban planning, reaching its peak under
Krishna Devaraya; it became one of the world's largest cities before
being sacked and abandoned after the Battle of Talikota in 1565, leaving
behind vast ruins now a UNESCO World Heritage site, showcasing grand
palaces, religious structures, and unique boulder-strewn landscapes.

Founding (1336): Established by Harihara and Bukka
(brothers from the Sangama dynasty) as Vijayanagara, meaning "City of Victory".

Location

Hampi is situated on the banks of the Tungabhadra River
in the eastern part of central Karnataka near the state border
with Andhra Pradesh. It is 140 kilometres (87 mi) southeast of
the Badami and Aihole archaeological sites
